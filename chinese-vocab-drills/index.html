Section 1: Chinese → English

Look at the Hanzi. Choose the correct English meaning. Use the Pinyin toggle if you need help.

1. 先生 🔊

► Pinyin
xiānsheng
► Answer
B. Mr./sir/husband — 先生 (xiānsheng) is a respectful term for a man; it also means 'husband' in Mainland Chinese usage. Don't confuse with 师傅 (shīfu), which is used for skilled workers or drivers.

2. 很多 🔊

► Pinyin
hěn duō
► Answer
A. many/a lot of — 很多 (hěn duō) literally is 'very much/many'. Used before nouns: 很多人 = many people, 很多时间 = a lot of time.

3. 不客气 🔊

► Pinyin
bù kèqi
► Answer
C. you're welcome — 不客气 (bù kèqi) literally means 'don't be polite'. A common response to 谢谢. Also used as 'please help yourself'.

4. 师傅 🔊

► Pinyin
shīfu
► Answer
D. master/skilled worker — 师傅 (shīfu) is a respectful address for someone with skill — taxi drivers, chefs, craftsmen. Don't confuse with 先生, used for professional men.

5. 还是 🔊

► Pinyin
háishi
► Answer
B. or (in questions) / still — 还是 (háishi) has two uses: (1) 'or' in A-还是-B questions (你要茶还是咖啡？), and (2) 'still/nevertheless'. Compare with 或者 (or in statements).
Section 2: English → Chinese

Read the English. Choose the correct Hanzi. Use 🔊 on each option to hear it. Toggle ► Pinyin to reveal readings.

1. (general measure word)

► Pinyin
A. 个 — gè
B. 年 — nián
C. 妻子 — qīzi
D. 呀 — ya
► Answer
A. 个 (gè) — the most common measure word in Chinese, used for people, objects, and many other nouns when no more specific measure word applies. 一个人 = one person.

2. year

► Pinyin
A. 妻子 — qīzi
B. 年 — nián
C. 了 — le
D. 个 — gè
► Answer
B. 年 (nián) — year. Used in: 今年 (this year), 明年 (next year), 去年 (last year), 一年 (one year).

3. wife

► Pinyin
A. 呀 — ya
B. 了 — le
C. 妻子 — qīzi
D. 年 — nián
► Answer
C. 妻子 (qīzi) — formal word for wife. The more colloquial terms are 老婆 and 太太. Note: 丈夫 = husband.

4. (completion/change-of-state particle)

► Pinyin
A. 年 — nián
B. 个 — gè
C. 呀 — ya
D. 了 — le
► Answer
D. 了 (le) — a particle indicating completed action or a new situation. 吃了 = ate/have eaten; 下雨了 = it's raining now (new state). Don't confuse with 呀 (exclamatory particle).

5. (softening/exclamatory particle)

► Pinyin
A. 呀 — ya
B. 妻子 — qīzi
C. 了 — le
D. 个 — gè
► Answer
A. 呀 (ya) — a softening/exclamatory particle, variant of 啊 used after vowels a/e/i/o/ü. Adds warmth or surprise: 好呀！(Great!) 走呀！(Let's go!)
Section 3: Fill in the Blank

Choose the word that best completes the sentence. Use 🔊 to hear the full sentence with the correct answer filled in.

1. 我______我的家人。 🔊

► Pinyin
Wǒ ______ wǒ de jiārén。
► Answer
B. 爱 — 我爱我的家人。
Wǒ ài wǒ de jiārén。
I love my family. — 爱 (ài) means to love. In Chinese, 爱 expresses deep love. For milder liking, use 喜欢.

2. 请______，里面有位子。 🔊

► Pinyin
Qǐng ______，lǐmiàn yǒu wèizi。
► Answer
A. 进去 — 请进去，里面有位子。
Qǐng jìnqù，lǐmiàn yǒu wèizi。
Please come in, there are seats inside. — 进去 = go in/enter (towards the inside). The opposite is 出去 (go out). 里面 = inside.

3. 这个商店______新鲜水果。 🔊

► Pinyin
Zhège shāngdiàn ______ xīnxiān shuǐguǒ。
► Answer
C. 卖 — 这个商店卖新鲜水果。
Zhège shāngdiàn mài xīnxiān shuǐguǒ。
This shop sells fresh fruit. — 卖 (mài, 4th tone) = to sell. Don't confuse with 买 (mǎi, 3rd tone) = to buy! The tones are different.

4. ______问，洗手间在哪里？ 🔊

► Pinyin
______ wèn，xǐshǒujiān zài nǎlǐ？
► Answer
C. 请 — 请问，洗手间在哪里？
Qǐng wèn，xǐshǒujiān zài nǎlǐ？
Excuse me, where is the restroom? — 请问 (qǐng wèn) is a fixed phrase for 'excuse me / may I ask'. 请 alone means 'please' or 'to invite'.

5. ______好！______是我的老师。 🔊

► Pinyin
______ hǎo！______ shì wǒ de lǎoshī。
► Answer
A. 您 — 您好！您是我的老师。
Nín hǎo！Nín shì wǒ de lǎoshī。
Hello! You are my teacher. — 您 (nín) is the polite/respectful form of 你 (you). Use it with elders, strangers, and in formal settings.
Section 4: English → Chinese Sentence Translation

Read the English sentence. Write the Chinese translation on paper or in your head, then toggle the answer. Use 🔊 next to the Chinese answer to hear it.

1. Translate: "Do you want tea or coffee?"

► Answer
你想要茶还是咖啡？ 🔊
Nǐ xiǎng yào chá háishi kāfēi？
Do you want tea or coffee?
还是 (háishi) is used in A-or-B questions. For 'or' in statements (not questions), use 或者 instead.

2. Translate: "My daughter is eight years old."

► Answer
我的女儿八岁了。 🔊
Wǒ de nǚér bā suì le。
My daughter is eight years old.
岁 (suì) = years old (for age of people). No need for 是 or 有: just say 八岁. Adding 了 signals a change (she has now turned 8).

3. Translate: "You can call me or send me a message."

► Answer
你可以给我打电话或者发信息。 🔊
Nǐ kěyǐ gěi wǒ dǎ diànhuà huòzhě fā xìnxi。
You can call me or send me a message.
或者 (huòzhě) = or, used in statements. Compare with 还是, which is for A-or-B choice questions.

4. Translate: "Can I have two cups of tea?"

► Answer
我可以要两杯茶吗？ 🔊
Wǒ kěyǐ yào liǎng bēi chá ma？
Can I have two cups of tea?
杯 (bēi) is both the measure word for cups/glasses AND the noun for cup. 一杯水 = a glass of water. Note: 两 (liǎng) is used for 'two' before measure words, not 二.

5. Translate: "My friend is at the restaurant right now."

► Answer
我的朋友现在在餐厅。 🔊
Wǒ de péngyou xiànzài zài cāntīng。
My friend is at the restaurant right now.
在 (zài) = to be located at. 现在 = now. The sentence has 现在在 — 'now at' — which looks like a repeat but they serve different roles: 现在 is a time word, 在 is a preposition.
