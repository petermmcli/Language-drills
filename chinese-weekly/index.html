Grammar

1. V + 得 (de) — Degree Complement: Describing How an Action Is Performed

Structure: Subject + Verb + 得 + adjective (no object); Subject + Verb + Object + Verb + 得 + adjective (with object).

Function: 得 (neutral tone "de") links a verb to a complement describing how well or to what degree an action is performed. When a direct object is present, the verb must be repeated before 得. Don't confuse with 的 (possessive) or 地 (adverbial). Common in habit and ability questions.

你起床起得早吗？🔊
Nǐ qǐ chuáng qǐ de zǎo ma？
Do you get up early?
他说中文说得很好。🔊
Tā shuō Zhōng wén shuō de hěn hǎo。
He speaks Chinese very well.
她唱歌唱得不错。🔊
Tā chàng gē chàng de bù cuò。
She sings quite well.
你做饭做得怎么样？🔊
Nǐ zuò fàn zuò de zěn me yàng？
How well do you cook?

2. 要 vs 会 — Planned Future vs Possibility/Capability

Structure: Subject + 要 + Verb (planned/intended); Subject + 会 + Verb (possible/capable).

Function: Both can express future actions, but 要 (yào) marks something already planned or decided. 会 (huì) marks prediction, likelihood, or a learned skill. Memory trick: 要 = "I'm going to" (committed plan); 会 = "I will/might/can" (probability or ability).

我明天要去上海。🔊
Wǒ míng tiān yào qù Shàng hǎi。
I'm going to Shanghai tomorrow. (planned intention)
明天会下雨。🔊
Míng tiān huì xià yǔ。
It will probably rain tomorrow. (prediction)
我要去超市买东西。🔊
Wǒ yào qù chāo shì mǎi dōng xi。
I'm going to the supermarket to buy things. (plan)
她会弹钢琴。🔊
Tā huì tán gāng qín。
She can play the piano. (learned skill)
