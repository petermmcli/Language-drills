Grammar

1. 栋 vs 家 — Buildings vs Businesses

Both count things related to buildings, but 栋 (dòng) targets the physical structure and 家 (jiā) targets the business or organization. 一栋酒店 = the hotel building; 一家酒店 = the hotel as a business. For "three restaurants" say 三家餐厅, never 三栋餐厅.

这条街上有三家银行。🔊
Zhè tiáo jiē shàng yǒu sān jiā yínháng.
There are three banks on this street.
学校里有五栋楼。🔊
Xuéxiào lǐ yǒu wǔ dòng lóu.
There are five buildings on campus.
那是一栋大楼，里面有一家餐厅。🔊
Nà shì yí dòng dàlóu, lǐmiàn yǒu yì jiā cāntīng.
That's a building, and inside is a restaurant.

2. 其他 vs 别的 — Formal vs Conversational "Other"

其他 (qí tā) is more formal and refers to the rest of a group — "the remaining ones." 别的 (bié de) is conversational and emphasizes "a different one." 其他 leans group-oriented; 别的 leans contrastive. Use 其他 in writing; reach for 别的 in casual speech.

还有其他问题吗？🔊
Hái yǒu qí tā wèntí ma?
Any other (remaining) questions?
我不想喝咖啡，我想喝别的。🔊
Wǒ bù xiǎng hē kāfēi, wǒ xiǎng hē bié de.
I don't want coffee; I want something else.
其他学生都来了，只有他没来。🔊
Qí tā xuésheng dōu lái le, zhǐ yǒu tā méi lái.
All the other students came — only he didn't.

3. 大小 / 尺寸 / 号 — Three Ways to Say "Size"

Choose based on context: 大小 (dàxiǎo) = general/abstract size comparison (literally "big-small"). 尺寸 (chǐcun) = specific measurements (clothing, room dimensions). 号 (hào) = labeled shopping sizes S/M/L, combined with 小/中/大 as 小号, 中号, 大号.

你穿什么号？🔊
Nǐ chuān shénme hào?
What size do you wear?
这两个盒子的大小一样。🔊
Zhè liǎng ge hézi de dàxiǎo yíyàng.
These two boxes are the same size.
请告诉我你的尺寸，我帮你找。🔊
Qǐng gàosu wǒ nǐ de chǐcun, wǒ bāng nǐ zhǎo.
Tell me your measurements; I'll help you find it.

4. 忘 / 记得 / 记住 — Forget / Remember / Memorize

Three points on the memory spectrum. 忘 (wàng) = forget (+ 了). 记得 (jìde) = remember/recall (everyday). 记住 (jìzhù) = resultative — the action is complete and firmly locked in memory. 请记住 = "please remember this" (imperative).

我忘了他的名字。🔊
Wǒ wàng le tā de míngzi.
I forgot his name.
我记得他的名字。🔊
Wǒ jìde tā de míngzi.
I remember his name.
我记住了你的电话号码。🔊
Wǒ jìzhù le nǐ de diànhuà hàomǎ.
I've memorized your phone number.

5. 刚 vs 刚才 — Adverb vs Time Noun for "Just Now"

刚 (gāng) is an adverb placed after the subject, before the verb. Time is subjective — could be minutes or months if it "feels" recent. Usually no 了. 刚才 (gāngcái) is a time noun like 今天 — goes before or after subject. Refers to a literal "just now" (within ~30 min). Often pairs with 的 in completed-past sentences.

我刚到。🔊
Wǒ gāng dào.
I just arrived. (adverb, no 了)
我刚才到的。🔊
Wǒ gāngcái dào de.
I just arrived a moment ago. (time noun, 的 marks past)
他刚才打电话给我了。🔊
Tā gāngcái dǎ diànhuà gěi wǒ le.
He called me just a moment ago.

6. 把 Construction — Subject + 把 + Object + Verb + Result

把 (bǎ) foregrounds the object as the thing being acted on and emphasizes the result or disposition of that object. Almost always needs a result complement, directional complement, or 了 after the verb — bare verbs sound incomplete. Use when the object is specific and definite.

我把前四节课备完了。🔊
Wǒ bǎ qián sì jié kè bèi wán le.
I finished prepping the first four lessons.
请把门关上。🔊
Qǐng bǎ mén guānshàng.
Please close the door.
我把手机忘在家里了。🔊
Wǒ bǎ shǒujī wàng zài jiā lǐ le.
I left my phone at home.

7. 过 (guò) — Experiential Aspect

过 (guò) after a verb marks that an action is part of the subject's past experience — "have (ever) done" at least once, time unspecified. It answers questions about life experience, not specific events. Think: English "have ever done / have done before."

我看过一部咏春的音乐剧。🔊
Wǒ kàn guo yí bù Yǒngchūn de yīnyuèjù.
I have seen a Wing Chun musical (before).
你去过中国吗？🔊
Nǐ qù guo Zhōngguó ma?
Have you ever been to China?
我没吃过芒果。🔊
Wǒ méi chī guo mángguǒ.
I have never eaten mango.

8. 了 / 完 / 到 — Completed Event / Endpoint / Achieved Goal

了 (le) marks a specific event as complete ("that thing happened"). 完 (wán) = the action went all the way to its endpoint ("finished"). 到 (dào) = the action reached its target or goal ("managed to get"). Same scenario, three different lenses.

我看了一部咏春的音乐剧。🔊
Wǒ kàn le yí bù Yǒngchūn de yīnyuèjù.
I watched a Wing Chun musical (that time; it happened).
我把音乐剧《咏春》看完了。🔊
Wǒ bǎ yīnyuèjù 《Yǒngchūn》 kàn wán le.
I finished watching the Wing Chun musical (start to end).
我终于买到那部音乐剧的票了。🔊
Wǒ zhōngyú mǎi dào nà bù yīnyuèjù de piào le.
I finally managed to buy tickets for that musical.

9. 得 / 必须 / 一定要 — Everyday / Rule-based / Emphatic Must

得 (děi) = casual "have to" driven by practical circumstances. 必须 (bìxū) = strong obligation, rule- or authority-based (formal). 一定要 (yídìng yào) = combines 一定 (definitely) + 要, strong emotional insistence or urgent advice.

我得走了。🔊
Wǒ děi zǒu le.
I have to go (practical need).
你必须按时交作业。🔊
Nǐ bìxū ànshí jiāo zuòyè.
You must turn in homework on time (rule).
你一定要小心。🔊
Nǐ yídìng yào xiǎoxīn.
You absolutely must be careful (insistence).

11. 多 + Verb / Verb + 得 + 多 — Do More vs Does a Lot

多 + Verb = imperative advice to do more of an action ("drink more water"). Verb + 得 + 多 = descriptive — reports how much someone does ("he eats a lot"). In 多+Verb, 多 directs; in Verb+得+多, 多 describes.

你应该多喝水，多休息。🔊
Nǐ yīnggāi duō hē shuǐ, duō xiūxi.
You should drink more water and rest more.
他吃得多。🔊
Tā chī de duō.
He eats a lot.
我睡得不多。🔊
Wǒ shuì de bù duō.
I don't sleep much.

12. Verb + 起来 + Adj — Sensory Impression

Verb + 起来 + adjective turns a sensory verb into a description of an impression: 看起来 (looks), 吃起来 (tastes), 听起来 (sounds). The adjective follows. Used for hedged judgments — what something appears to be, not what it definitively is.

这个蛋糕看起来很好吃。🔊
Zhège dàngāo kàn qilai hěn hǎochī.
This cake looks delicious.
这个苹果吃起来很甜。🔊
Zhège píngguǒ chī qilai hěn tián.
This apple tastes sweet.
这个主意听起来不错。🔊
Zhège zhǔyi tīng qilai búcuò.
That idea sounds good.

13. 像…一样 — Just Like / Similar To

像…一样 brackets a noun to express similarity. Add an adjective/verb after to specify the dimension ("as X as Y"), or use standalone ("just like X"). Works for taste, appearance, behavior, or anything.

这个肉吃起来像鸡肉一样。🔊
Zhège ròu chī qilai xiàng jīròu yíyàng.
This meat tastes like chicken.
他像他爸爸一样高。🔊
Tā xiàng tā bàba yíyàng gāo.
He is as tall as his dad.
这家水果店的苹果像超市一样新鲜。🔊
Zhè jiā shuǐguǒdiàn de píngguǒ xiàng chāoshì yíyàng xīnxiān.
The apples at this fruit shop are as fresh as a supermarket's.

14. 是不是 — Tag Question / Confirmation Seeker

是不是 literally means "is or is not." Drop it before the verb (or at sentence end) to create a confirmation-seeking question — "Am I right that…?" / "...right?" Implies you suspect the answer is yes; you're checking, not asking from zero. Can sit before the verb or at the end.

你是不是会说中文？🔊
Nǐ shì bú shì huì shuō Zhōngwén?
You can speak Chinese, right?
这个菜辣，是不是？🔊
Zhège cài là, shì bú shì?
This dish is spicy, isn't it?
你是不是要买苹果？🔊
Nǐ shì bú shì yào mǎi píngguǒ?
You want to buy apples, right?

15. 每…都… — Every … Without Exception

每 (měi) introduces "every," and 都 (dōu) is almost always required later to mark "all without exception." Forgetting 都 is a classic learner mistake. With time words (每天, 每年, 每个星期), 都 follows the subject and precedes the verb.

我每天都喝咖啡。🔊
Wǒ měi tiān dōu hē kāfēi.
I drink coffee every day.
每个学生都要做作业。🔊
Měi ge xuésheng dōu yào zuò zuòyè.
Every student has to do homework.
每个礼拜我都去市场买水果。🔊
Měi ge lǐbài wǒ dōu qù shìchǎng mǎi shuǐguǒ.
Every week I go to the market to buy fruit.

16. Time period + V + Obj + Number + 次 — Frequency Pattern

Express how many times an action occurs: time period first, then the verb, then number + 次. For separable verbs like 游泳, insert 次 between them: 游三次泳, not ~~游泳三次~~. The time period (每天/一个礼拜/每年) can vary but the rest stays the same.

我一个礼拜游三次泳。🔊
Wǒ yí gè lǐbài yóu sān cì yǒng.
I swim three times a week.
每年我去三次香港。🔊
Měi nián wǒ qù sān cì Xiānggǎng.
I go to Hong Kong three times a year.
我每天练习两次听说读写。🔊
Wǒ měi tiān liànxí liǎng cì tīng shuō dú xiě.
I practice the four skills twice a day.
