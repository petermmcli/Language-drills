Grammar

1. Adjective + 一点儿 — "a bit more [adjective]"

Place 一点儿 (yī diǎnr) directly after an adjective to mean "a bit more / a little [adjective]." Common in shopping requests when asking for a slightly different version. Structure: Adjective + 一点儿. Note: 一点儿 sits after the adjective; it does NOT mean "a small amount" (that's 一点 in a different position).

我想要大一点儿的衣服。🔊
Wǒ xiǎng yào dà yī diǎn r de yī fu。
I'd like a slightly bigger piece of clothing.
我想要小一点儿的衣服。🔊
Wǒ xiǎng yào xiǎo yī diǎn r de yī fu。
I'd like a slightly smaller piece of clothing.
便宜一点儿。🔊
Pián yi yī diǎn r。
A little cheaper, please.

2. 想 + Verb VS 想 + Noun — Two completely different meanings

想 (xiǎng) changes meaning depending on what follows it. Before a verb it means "want to / would like to do." Before a noun (especially a person or place) it means "miss / think of." Structure: 想 + Verb = want to do | 想 + Noun = miss / think of.

我想喝水。🔊
Wǒ xiǎng hē shuǐ。
I want to drink water. (想 + verb)
我想去中国。🔊
Wǒ xiǎng qù Zhōng guó。
I want to go to China. (想 + verb)
我想你。🔊
Wǒ xiǎng nǐ。
I miss you. (想 + noun)
他想家。🔊
Tā xiǎng jiā。
He misses home. (想 + noun)

3. 有 + size/color + 的吗？ — "Do you have one in [size/color]?"

A core shopping question pattern. Drop the noun being shopped for after 有 (yǒu) and instead use a description (size, color, style) followed by 的 to nominalize the description, then close with 吗 to make it a yes/no question. Structure: S + 有 + [description] + 的 + 吗?

有别的颜色的吗？🔊
Yǒu bié de yán sè de ma？
Do you have it in another color?
有大一点儿的吗？🔊
Yǒu dà yī diǎn r de ma？
Do you have a slightly bigger one?
有黄色的吗？🔊
Yǒu huáng sè de ma？
Do you have it in yellow?
有便宜一点儿的吗？🔊
Yǒu pián yi yī diǎn r de ma？
Do you have a cheaper one?

4. 楼 vs 层 — "the place" vs "the counting unit"

Both relate to floors of a building, but they point at different things. 楼 (lóu) = the place / a named floor — "what you walk into." 层 (céng) = the counting unit / level — "how you count it." Patterns: [number] + 楼 ("on the Nth floor") · 这栋楼有 [number] 层 ("this building has N floors"). 第 + number + 层 = the Nth level (more formal/descriptive).

三楼。🔊
Sān lóu。
Third floor. (the place you go)
这栋楼有十层。🔊
Zhè dòng lóu yǒu shí céng。
This building has 10 floors.
第一层。🔊
Dì yī céng。
The first level. (formal)
试衣间在二楼。🔊
Shì yī jiān zài èr lóu。
The fitting room is on the second floor.

5. 再 vs 又 — Forward-looking vs backward-looking repetition (review)

再 (zài) always points forward — future repetition or "not until then." 又 (yòu) always confirms something already happened — past repetition, often with a hint of annoyance. Memory trick: 再 = "next time", 又 = "one more time (already)".

我再去一次。🔊
Wǒ zài qù yí cì。
I'll go again. (haven't gone yet)
我又去了一次。🔊
Wǒ yòu qù le yí cì。
I went again. (already did it)
你再说一遍。🔊
Nǐ zài shuō yí biàn。
Say it again. (request)
你又说一遍。🔊
Nǐ yòu shuō yí biàn。
You said it again. (complaint / observation)
又下雨了。🔊
Yòu xià yǔ le。
It's raining again.
明天我再给你打电话。🔊
Míng tiān wǒ zài gěi nǐ dǎ diàn huà。
I'll call you again tomorrow.

6. 以后 vs 然后 — "in the future / after that" vs "and then" (review)

以后 (yǐhòu) = "after (a point)" or "from now on / in the future" — refers to a time after a reference point. 然后 (ránhòu) = "then" — the next step in a sequence. Use 以后 for time relationships; use 然后 for step-by-step sequencing.

吃饭以后去。🔊
Chī fàn yǐ hòu qù。
Go after eating. (time relationship)
以后我每天学习。🔊
Yǐ hòu wǒ měi tiān xué xí。
From now on, I study every day.
我吃饭，然后喝咖啡。🔊
Wǒ chī fàn， rán hòu hē kā fēi。
I eat, then drink coffee. (step-by-step)
我吃饭以后喝咖啡。🔊
Wǒ chī fàn yǐ hòu hē kā fēi。
I drink coffee after eating. (time relationship)

7. 一个 ... 另一个 — "one ... the other" (fixed pairing)

Structure: 一个 + (Noun/Verb phrase) + 另一个

Function: Used when describing two specific members of a defined pair, where one acts on or relates to the other.

Note: Don't substitute 别的 (biéde); 别的 means "others in general" — vague and open-ended, which breaks the pairing logic.

一个告诉另一个。🔊
Yí ge gào su lìng yí ge.
One tells the other.
一个在前，另一个在后。🔊
Yí ge zài qián, lìng yí ge zài hòu.
One is in front, the other is behind.
