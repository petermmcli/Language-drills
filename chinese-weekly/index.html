Grammar

1. V + 起来 + adj — Perception-based inference

Attach 起来 (qǐlái) after a perception verb to describe how something seems based on a sense. Common pairs: 听起来 (sounds), 看起来 (looks), 闻起来 (smells), 吃起来 (tastes). The adjective follows. Structure: Subject + V + 起来 + adj. New pattern G103.

听起来不错。🔊
Tīng qǐlái búcuò。
Sounds good.
他看起来很累。🔊
Tā kàn qǐlái hěn lèi。
He looks very tired.
这个菜吃起来很辣。🔊
Zhè ge cài chī qǐlái hěn là。
This dish tastes very spicy.

3. 就 (jiù) — "only / just" (limiting)

Used before a small quantity or restricted item to mean "only" or "just." Structure: Subject + 就 + [small quantity / item]. Compare with 只 (zhǐ), which is more neutral; this 就 can carry a slight tone of "that is all there is."

我就有五块钱。🔊
Wǒ jiù yǒu wǔ kuài qián。
I only have five dollars.
今天就三个人来。🔊
Jīntiān jiù sān ge rén lái。
Only three people came today.

4. 就 (jiù) — emphasis ("exactly / simply / stubbornly")

Adds emphatic or stubborn insistence to the verb — "I just / I simply / I exactly do this." Structure: Subject + 就 + Verb / Verb phrase. Common in arguments or strong assertions; in negative sentences (我就不去) it conveys defiant refusal.

我就喜欢这个。🔊
Wǒ jiù xǐhuan zhège。
I just like this one.
我就不去。🔊
Wǒ jiù bú qù。
I am simply not going.

5. 就 (jiù) — "earlier / sooner than expected"

When 就 follows a time expression it signals that an action happened earlier or more readily than expected. Structure: Time expression + 就 + Verb + 了. Compare 才 (cái), which marks the opposite — later than expected. Memory aid: 就 = sooner; 才 = later.

他六点就到了。🔊
Tā liù diǎn jiù dào le。
He arrived as early as six o'clock.
我八点就睡觉了。🔊
Wǒ bā diǎn jiù shuìjiào le。
I went to bed as early as eight.

6. 边 vs 面 (direction suffixes)

Most basic directions (上下前后里外) accept BOTH suffixes interchangeably — 上边/上面, 下边/下面, 前边/前面, 后边/后面, 里边/里面, 外边/外面. Lateral and demonstrative directions take only 边: 左边, 右边, 旁边, 这边, 那边, 哪边, 两边 — never 左面/右面. Two specific words go only with 面: 对面 ("opposite") and 面前 ("in front of someone"). Memory aid: 边 = sides/edges; 面 = surfaces/faces.

银行在学校对面。🔊
Yínháng zài xuéxiào duìmiàn。
The bank is across from the school.

7. 没 + Verb — Past negation (without 了)

Use 没 (méi) before a verb to negate a past or completed action. Structure: Subject + 没 + Verb (+ Object). Because 没 already strongly marks non-completion, sentence-final 了 is omitted — adding 了 shifts the meaning to change of state (see pattern 8).

我没看见他。🔊
Wǒ méi kàn jiàn tā。
I didn't see him.
她没吃早饭。🔊
Tā méi chī zǎofàn。
She didn't eat breakfast.

8. 没 + Verb + 了 — Change of state ("no longer / can't anymore")

When sentence-final 了 is added to a 没 sentence, the meaning shifts from simple past negation to a change of state: the subject can no longer do something that was previously possible. Structure: Subject + 没 + Verb (+ Object) + 了. Most natural when context establishes what was possible before.

现在我没看见他了。🔊
Xiàn zài wǒ méi kàn jiàn tā le。
Now I can't see him anymore.
天太黑了，我没看见路了。🔊
Tiān tài hēi le， wǒ méi kàn jiàn lù le。
It's too dark; I can't see the road anymore.

9. 再 vs 就 in sequential directions — continuation vs. simplicity

Both 再 and 就 precede a verb in directions, but they carry different meanings. 再 (zài) means "then continue / take the next step" — it implies adding more motion or an additional step. 就 (jiù) means "just / simply" — it signals the action is the only thing to do, reassuring the listener that no extra effort is needed. Structure: … 再 + Verb (another step) or … 就 + Verb (that's all you need).

先一直走，到红绿灯那儿，再直走两分钟。🔊
Xiān yì zhí zǒu， dào hóng lǜ dēng nàr， zài zhí zǒu liǎng fēn zhōng。
First go straight; when you reach the traffic light, then go straight for another two minutes.
出了地铁站，你就直走。🔊
Chū le dì tiě zhàn， nǐ jiù zhí zǒu。
After you come out of the subway station, just go straight.

10. 你是说…… — paraphrasing and checking understanding

Use 你是说 + [your interpretation] to paraphrase or confirm what someone just said, equivalent to "you mean..." or "are you saying that...". Leave it trailing with "……" to invite confirmation. For a softer version, prefix with 听起来 (tīng qǐ lái, "it sounds like"). Structure: 你是说 + [interpretation].

你是说你明天不来了？🔊
Nǐ shì shuō nǐ míng tiān bù lái le？
You mean you're not coming tomorrow?
听起来，你是说价格太贵了。🔊
Tīng qǐ lái， nǐ shì shuō jià gé tài guì le。
It sounds like you're saying the price is too expensive.

11. Time-word placement in travel sentences — before or after subject

In Chinese travel (and activity) sentences, time words can appear either before or after the subject — both orders are grammatically correct. Placing the time word before the subject emphasizes when; placing it after is more neutral. Structure: Subject + Time Word + Verb + Destination OR Time Word + Subject + Verb + Destination.

我下个星期去香港。🔊
Wǒ xià ge xīng qī qù Xiāng gǎng。
I'll go to Hong Kong next week.
上个星期我去北京了。🔊
Shàng ge xīng qī wǒ qù Běi jīng le。
Last week I went to Beijing.
