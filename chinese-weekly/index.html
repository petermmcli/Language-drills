Grammar

1. 再 vs 就 — Next step vs. immediate reassurance

Both 再 and 就 can link a following action to a condition, but they carry different nuances in directions. Structure: … 再 + Verb (then take the next step) / … 就 + Verb (just do this; all you need to do).

再 (zài) says “then proceed to the next step — there is still more to do.” 就 (jiù) reassures the listener that the action is the only or final thing needed. Use 再直走 in multi-step directions; use 就直走 to reassure someone that going straight is all it takes.

到红绿灯那儿，再直走两分钟。🔊
Dào hónglǜdēng nàr, zài zhí zǒu liǎng fēnzhōng.
When you reach the traffic light, then continue straight for two minutes. (more steps ahead)
到红绿灯那儿，就直走两分钟。🔊
Dào hónglǜdēng nàr, jiù zhí zǒu liǎng fēnzhōng.
Once you reach the traffic light, just keep going straight for two minutes. (that’s all)
先左转，再右转，就到了。🔊
Xiān zuǒ zhuǎn, zài yòu zhuǎn, jiù dào le.
First turn left, then turn right, and you’ll be there.

2. 先……再…… — First… then… (sequencing steps)

The standard frame for giving directions or explaining a step-by-step procedure. Structure: 先 + Verb1，再 + Verb2.

先 (xiān) marks the first action and 再 (zài) marks the following one, giving a clear “first this, then that” ordering. The default pattern in navigation. Contrast 然后: more narrative. Contrast 就: adds immediacy, not sequence.

先左转，再右转。🔊
Xiān zuǒ zhuǎn, zài yòu zhuǎn.
First turn left, then turn right.
先一直走，再到红绿灯那儿右转。🔊
Xiān yìzhí zǒu, zài dào hónglǜdēng nàr yòu zhuǎn.
First walk straight, then turn right at the traffic light.
先吃饭，再看电视。🔊
Xiān chī fàn, zài kàn diànshì.
First eat, then watch TV.

4. Verb/Time + 以前/以后 — Before / After

Structure: Verb phrase or Time + 以前 (before) / 以后 (after). Colloquial 前/后 and formal 之前/之后 work the same way.
Key rule: 以前/以后 come AFTER the event they anchor to — opposite English order. "Before eating" = 吃饭以前, NOT 以前吃饭.

我会在十二点以前到。🔊
Wǒ huì zài shí'èr diǎn yǐqián dào.
I'll arrive before noon.
我吃完饭以后去书店找你。🔊
Wǒ chī wán fàn yǐhòu qù shūdiàn zhǎo nǐ.
After I finish eating I'll go find you at the bookstore.

5. 再 + Verb vs 又 + Verb — Again: Future vs. Past

Structure: Subject + 再 + Verb (future / not-yet repetition) vs Subject + 又 + Verb + 了 (already-happened repetition).
再 (zài) = "again" for something not yet done — plans, requests, next steps. 又 (yòu) = "again" for something already done, often with mild surprise or complaint. Memory trick: 再 = next time; 又 = one more time (already).

然后再直走。🔊
Ránhòu zài zhí zǒu.
Then go straight again. (next step)
他又迟到了。🔊
Tā yòu chídào le.
He was late again. (already happened)

8. 事情 vs 东西 — Matter/Affair vs. Physical Thing

Structure: 事情 (shìqing) = an intangible matter, affair, or task to handle. 东西 (dōngxi) = a physical object you can hold or buy.
"I have things to do" → 事情. "I want to buy something" → 东西.

我今天有很多事情。🔊
Wǒ jīntiān yǒu hěn duō shìqing.
I have a lot of things to do today.
我现在出去买一点儿东西。🔊
Wǒ xiànzài chūqù mǎi yìdiǎnr dōngxi.
I'm going out to buy a few things now.
