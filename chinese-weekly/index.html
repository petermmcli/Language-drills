Grammar

1. Prices in formal Chinese — 元 / 角 system

In formal or written Chinese, prices use 元 (yuán) for the base unit and 角 (jiǎo) for tenths (1/10 of a yuan). Structure: number + 元 + number + 角. A third unit, 分 (fēn), represents hundredths (1/100 yuan) and can follow 角 when needed. This register appears on receipts, price tags, invoices, and official documents.

一共是十五元五角。🔊
Yígòng shì shíwǔ yuán wǔ jiǎo.
The total is fifteen yuan and five jiao.
这支笔三元二角。🔊
Zhè zhī bǐ sān yuán èr jiǎo.
This pen is three yuan two jiao.
票价是二十元八角。🔊
Piàojià shì èrshí yuán bā jiǎo.
The ticket price is twenty yuan eight jiao.

2. Prices in spoken Chinese — 块 / 毛 system

In everyday speech, prices use 块 (kuài) (colloquial for 元) and 毛 (máo) (colloquial for 角). Structure: number + 块 + number + 毛. The final unit is often dropped in rapid speech (e.g., 八块五 for 八块五毛). 块/毛 are used when shopping, ordering food, or talking about money casually. The amounts are identical to 元/角 — only the register differs.

这个西瓜十块钱。🔊
Zhège xīguā shí kuài qián.
This watermelon is ten kuai.
一共是六块八毛。🔊
Yígòng shì liù kuài bā máo.
The total is six kuai eight mao.
苹果两块五一斤。🔊
Píngguǒ liǎng kuài wǔ yī jīn.
Apples are two kuai fifty per jin.

3. 着 (zhe) — persistent state (V + 着)

Attach 着 (zhe) after a verb to show the action's resulting state is ongoing. Unlike 在 (action in progress), 着 marks a static state — wearing, holding, being open. Negative: 没 + Verb + 着. Common with 穿, 拿, 开, 站, 坐.

他穿着白色的衬衫。🔊
Tā chuān zhe báisè de chènshān.
He's wearing a white shirt.
门开着呢。🔊
Mén kāi zhe ne.
The door is open.

4. Questions with V + 着 (吗 / 没有)

Add 吗 for a neutral yes/no question, or 没有 at the end for an A-not-A question asking whether the state holds. Both ask about the current state, not a completed action.

他穿着白色的衬衫吗？🔊
Tā chuān zhe báisè de chènshān ma?
Is he wearing a white shirt?
你拿着那本书没有？🔊
Nǐ ná zhe nà běn shū méiyǒu?
Are you holding that book or not?

5. Affirmative-negative questions (A 不 A)

Place the positive and negative of the same adjective or verb side by side: A + 不 + A. No 吗 needed. For two-syllable words, only the first syllable repeats (新不新鲜). The answer picks one side. Direct and neutral in tone.

这些苹果新不新鲜？🔊
Zhèxiē píngguǒ xīn bu xīnxiān?
Are these apples fresh or not?
你饿不饿？🔊
Nǐ è bu è?
Are you hungry or not?
